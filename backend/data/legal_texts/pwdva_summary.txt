Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act (PWDVA), 2005:
- Definition: Covers any harm or injury to the health, safety, life, limb, or well-being of a woman. Includes physical, sexual, verbal, emotional, and economic abuse.
- key-provision: Protection Officers (PO) are appointed to assist victims in filing Domestic Incident Reports (DIR).
- key-provision: Right to Residence (Section 17): A woman has the right to reside in the shared household, regardless of whether she has any legal title to it.
- key-provision: Protection Orders (Section 18): Court can prohibit the abuser from committing violence, entering the workplace/school, or communicating with the victim.
- key-provision: Monetary Relief (Section 20): Court can order the abuser to pay for medical expenses and loss of earnings.
- key-provision: Custody Orders (Section 21): Temporary custody of children to the victim.
- key-provision: Service Providers: NGOs and other organizations can register to provide aid, medical help, and shelter.

Indian Penal Code (IPC) Section 498A:
- Definition: Cruelty by husband or his relatives.
- nature: Cognizable, non-bailable, and non-compoundable offense.
- punishment: Imprisonment for a term which may extend to three years and shall also be liable to fine.
- cruelty-definition: Willful conduct likely to drive the woman to suicide or cause grave injury/danger to life/limb/health. Harassment for dowry.

Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961:
- prohibition: Giving or taking dowry is a crime.
- punishment: Imprisonment for not less than 5 years and fine.

Safety Tips:
- If you are in immediate danger, call 112 or 100 or 181 immediately.
- Try to go to a safe place like a neighbor's house or a public area.
- Keep a small bag packed with essential documents (ID, bank passbook, certificates) if possible.
